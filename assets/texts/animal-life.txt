With the Congo river, its rainforests, savanna, wetlands, lakes and mountains, the 
Democratic Republic of the Congo has one of the most diverse populations of plants and animals 
in the world. This includes many endangered and rare species, such as the gorilla and bonobo. 
There are known to be more than 10,000 species of plant, 600 timber species, 280 reptile species, 
400 mammal species, including the forest elephant, gorilla, forest buffalo, bongo, and okapi, 
and 1,000 bird species.
